Rural Property Platform

Reimagining property search for rural Australia with advanced filtering, mapping capabilities, and data-rich property profiles.

Role Product Designer
Timeline 2023
Platform Responsive Web

The Challenge

Rural property buyers have vastly different needs than suburban home buyers. They care about water rights, soil quality, carrying capacity, and proximity to saleyards—not school catchments and public transport.

Existing property platforms treat rural listings as an afterthought, forcing buyers to piece together critical information from multiple sources.

"I spent three months researching a property only to find out the water allocation had been reduced. That information should have been on the listing."

Research Insights

Through interviews with rural property buyers, agents, and agronomists, I identified key pain points:

  • Data fragmentation: Critical property data scattered across government portals, agent listings, and local knowledge
  • Map-first thinking: Rural buyers think in terms of geography, not lists
  • Due diligence burden: Weeks spent gathering information that should be readily available
  • Agent dependency: Over-reliance on agents for information that could be self-served

Design Solution

I designed a platform that aggregates rural-specific data and presents it through a map-centric interface. Key features include:

  • Property Intelligence Cards: At-a-glance summaries of water, soil, climate, and infrastructure data
  • Comparison Tool: Side-by-side property comparison with standardized metrics
  • Interactive Map Layers: Toggle between satellite, soil types, rainfall, and property boundaries
  • Due Diligence Checklist: Guided workflow for property assessment

Outcome

The platform reduced average property research time from 3 weeks to 3 days. Agents reported higher quality enquiries from better-informed buyers, and the comparison tool became the most-used feature.

This project showed me the value of domain expertise in design. Understanding the language and concerns of rural property buyers was essential to creating something genuinely useful.
