Project description

In a group project for 'The Village Project,' we redesigned confusing aspects of their website to enhance user understanding and increase membership sign-ups. Through surveys and feedback analysis, we identified key areas for improvement, resulting in the redesign of three main pages and the introduction of characters to guide users through the sign-up process more intuitively.

Revamping the
User Experience

The original design lacked affordances, which affected the users understanding of the business model and reduced the number of members signing up. To tackle this problem, my group brainstormed different ways we could improve the website to bring in more members.

Our first step was to survey over 150 people, including members and non-members, to understand which parts of the site were most important in bringing in more members. Based on this feedback, we focused on redesigning three main pages and introduced characters to create a story to guide users through the sign-up process more intuitively.

Unleashing User Engagement

Through our testing, We found through our interactive sign up process as well as the infographic style share economy page users were able to grasp the concept easily and also understand how to membership works.

As part of the team, I played a significant role in conceptualizing and prototyping, coordinating everyone's efforts and assets into a high-fidelity, functioning prototype on Figma. It was a great experience to lead the prototyping stage and see our conceptualized work come to life.

We went through several different iterations of how the users journey should work and visually what creates the most welcoming and comforting setting. Adding in different interactive elements such as the payment slider proved to be a great success.
